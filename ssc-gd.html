Constable (General Duty) Examination

Introduction

The Constable (General Duty) Examination conducted by the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) is a prestigious recruitment drive aimed at selecting candidates for the post of Constable in Central Armed Police Forces (CAPFs), National Investigation Agency (NIA), Secretariat Security Force (SSF), and Rifleman (GD) in Assam Rifles.

Examination Structure

The Constable (GD) examination typically comprises multiple stages:

  • Computer Based Examination (CBE): This stage assesses candidates' proficiency in subjects such as General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Knowledge & General Awareness, Elementary Mathematics, and English/Hindi language.
  • Physical Efficiency Test (PET): Candidates who qualify the CBE undergo PET, which evaluates their physical fitness through tasks such as running, long jump, and high jump.
  • Physical Standard Test (PST): Candidates who clear PET undergo PST, which verifies their physical measurements (height, chest, weight, etc.).
  • Medical Examination: Candidates who clear PST undergo a medical examination to ensure they meet the prescribed standards.
